# Milk Tea Base
This recipie is pure fiction, I made it up after looking at some youtube videos.

## Ingredients
- 450ml Water
- 150ml Milk of choice
- 6-8g loose tea leaves (1-2 bags) depending on desired strength
- medium to large icecubes
- Sweetener to taste (white cristallized sugar dissolves best but honey offers deeper flavours)

> [!Note] Milk:Tea ratio
> Keep the ratio of Milk:Water/Tea around ==1:3== try around with +-.1 and adjust as you like.
> This is a base to go off of not a law to follow.

## Recipie
**Equipment**
- 1 Fridge or cooler (not freezer)
- 1 Vessel with lid for cold brewing tea (eg.: empty Mason's-jar)

### Cold brewed tea
1. Put tea into brewing vessel
2. Add water until almost full
3. Close vessel and let chill in fridge overnight

### Assembly
1. Strain tea into mixing glass
2. Add cold milk
3. Add sweetener to taste
4. Mix until completely dissolved
5. Serve over ice cubes, preferably with straw

> ## [!Tip] Personal recommendations
> Milk:Tea Ratio of ~ 1:3.1
> Whole Milk
> Brew tea as stated
> 1 teaspoon (~3g) of honey to sweeten
> In tall glass with 2-3 icecubes large enough to stack instead of fitting in side by side
